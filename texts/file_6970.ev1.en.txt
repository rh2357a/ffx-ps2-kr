Players can learn techniques
from their opponents.
This is called {Yellow}techcopy{White}.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
First, have one of your
players {Yellow}mark{White} an opponent
who knows a desirable technique.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
Now that player will keep an
eye on that opponent in order
to learn his techniques.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
Select {Yellow}Break to Bickson{White} here.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
"{Yellow}Techcopy!{White}" appears at the top
of the screen when a player
uses a technique during a game.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
This indicates a window of opportunity
to learn that technique. Press {Button Circle} at
the right time in order to learn it.
Let's try it now.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
Just remember that the player
who learns the technique is not
necessarily the one it is used against.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
Players who have marked that opponent
are the ones who learn his techniques.
If multiple players have marked him,
only one will learn at any one time.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
Your players may have trouble learning
techniques, though, if they are of lower
levels than their respective targets.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
Once you've learned a new technique,
you can equip it at halftime or
at the start of the next match.

{WINDOW}
Normally the ball is thrown here.

{WINDOW}
But in an encounter, opposing
players try to {Yellow}block{White} your
passes and shots.

{WINDOW}
{Yellow}BL{White} represents a player's skill at blocking.
Here, Bickson's blocking stat is {Yellow}2{White}.

{WINDOW}
The pass loses an amount of PA
equal to Bickson's BL. The actual
calculation is slightly randomized.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
If PA falls to {Yellow}0{White} or less,
the ball is {Yellow}intercepted{White}
by the opposing player.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
In this example, PA is {Yellow}{VAR12:30}{White}, and BL is {Yellow}{VAR12:31}{White}.
The estimated result is {VAR12:30} - {VAR12:31} = {VAR12:32}.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
As long as PA is at least
{Yellow}1{White}, the pass goes through.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
Once through, the pass continues
to expend PA as it travels
toward its intended receiver.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
Watch PA closely
as we resume play.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
The defender reduced the PA of
the pass when he got a hand on it.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
Now let's see what happens when the
defender's BL stat is much higher.
--------------------------------------------------End--
{WINDOW}
This time, his
{Yellow}BL{White} is {Yellow}{VAR12:30}{White}.

{WINDOW}
